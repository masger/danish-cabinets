# Editor's Decision - Acceptance

**Manuscript ID**: BMJ-2024-XMAS-TRANSPORTMINISTER-R2
**Title**: "SKATTEMINISTER SYNDROME RECONSIDERED: Exploratory evidence that Transport, not Tax, may be the hazardous portfolio in Danish government"

---

Dear Authors,

I am pleased to inform you that your manuscript has been **accepted for publication** in the BMJ Christmas Issue 2024.

## Reviewer's Final Comments

Dr. Kritiskansen has provided the following remarks:

> "The authors have satisfactorily addressed all of my concerns. The revised title appropriately reflects the exploratory nature of the findings, the limitations are clearly stated, and the speculative discussion is now properly labeled.
>
> While the statistical evidence does not definitively debunk the 'Skatteminister Syndrome,' the paper provides valuable hypothesis-generating observations. The finding that Tax Ministers have among the lowest firing rates (11.8%)—contrary to folk wisdom—deserves attention in Danish political discourse.
>
> I recommend acceptance.
>
> P.S. — The butter cookies are not necessary, though I appreciate the sentiment. I remain skeptical of bribery attempts, even delicious ones."
